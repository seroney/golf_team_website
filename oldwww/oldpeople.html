Joe Kuchta

Hometown: Pennsylvania
Contacts: email

Joe Kuchta begins his fourth career (third at MIT) as he takes the reins of the MIT golf program this year. Joe succeeds his long time friend Jack Barry as golf coach. Jack spent 24 years at the helm of the program.
A native of Pennsylvania, Joe enrolled in the Navy out of high school. He spent 10 years in the service, a good portion of which was in the medical corps in the South Pacific during World War II. He attended Tufts University after his honorable discharge in 1948.
Following his graduation from Tufts in 1952, Joe was employed by MIT in the research department working on fuel elements for atomic submarines. His medical training led his supervisors to rely on him for first aid and safety concerns as the research progressed. He served many MIT departments and satellite facilities in a safety consulting capacity. He moved into the MIT Safety Office full-time in the early 1960's.
Joe retired from the Safety Office in 1986. To prepare for his "retirement", he took golf lessons from Jack Barry. Jack asked Joe if he would assist with the intercollegiate golf program and he has been the assistant coach for the last nine years. [Taken from MIT Sports Annual 1996-97]

John Zehren

Hometown: Fond du Lac, Wisconsin
High School: St Mary's Springs HS
MIT Class: Class of 1999
Designated Major: Geo-Physics
Contacts: email

"I've been playing golf since I was 10. My father started me off and it was a good way to spend time with him. One of my first memories of golf was he and I playing together, back when I still used his clubs. I played every year after that and then in high school I tried out for the team. When I was a sophomore I played jv and junior and senior year I played varsity. A lot of what I am today as a golfer is due to my high school coach Joe Bird. He honed my skills and my senior year is was second team all conference, first team academic all state, and we took 4th as a team in state in Wisconsin. After that I took a 2 year hiatus from golf to pursue a career in hockey. After those two years I came back to school and went to MIT. I was a little rusty after those two years but with the guidence of the MIT coach I am working back to my 10 handicap which I had at the end of my senior year. I plan to continue playing golf and hockey throughout my MIT career and hopefully become as major role in the golf team as I am on the hockey team."
